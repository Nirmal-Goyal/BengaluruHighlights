History of Chinnaswamy Stadium

Built in 1969, the M. Chinnaswamy Stadium is one of India's premier cricket venues. It is named after M. Chinnaswamy, who served as the President of the Board of Control for Cricket in India (BCCI). The stadium has hosted numerous iconic international matches and is home to the IPL team Royal Challengers Bangalore.

Main Attractions

The stadium is known for its electric atmosphere during IPL matches, especially when RCB plays. It also boasts eco-friendly initiatives like solar panels, lush green seating zones, and the iconic Pavilion End.
