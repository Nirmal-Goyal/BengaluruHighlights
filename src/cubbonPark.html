History of Cubbon Park

Established in 1870, Cubbon Park was initially named Meade's Park and later renamed after Sir Mark Cubbon, the commissioner of Mysore. It spans over 300 acres, serving as a green oasis in Bengaluru.

Main Attractions

The park is home to iconic landmarks like Attara Kacheri (High Court), Sheshadri Iyer Memorial Hall (State Library), and various statues. It also features a diverse collection of flora and fauna.

Activities at Cubbon Park

Visitors enjoy morning walks, cycling, picnics, and photography. The park also hosts cultural events and is a favorite spot for families and nature lovers.
