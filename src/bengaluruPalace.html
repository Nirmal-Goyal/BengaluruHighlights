History of Bengaluru Palace

Built in 1887, Bengaluru Palace was originally constructed by Rev. J. Garrett and later acquired by the Wadiyar family in 1920. The palace is a testament to the grandeur of royal life in Bangalore and reflects a blend of Tudor, Scottish Gothic, and Victorian architecture.

Architectural Highlights

The palace is renowned for its Tudor-style architecture with magnificent woodwork, arches, stained glass windows, and sprawling gardens. The interiors are lavish, with intricate wood carvings and vintage furniture that reflect the royal heritage.

Activities at Bengaluru Palace

Visitors can explore the royal interiors, enjoy the palace grounds, and even attend cultural events and concerts held in the palace premises. The palace also serves as a venue for various exhibitions and gatherings.
